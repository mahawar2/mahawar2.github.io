About Me

I am currently working as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning at Manipal University Jaipur. I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur in May 2026 under the supervision of Dr. Angshuman Paul. My doctoral research, titled "Towards Sustainable Deep Learning Methods for Chest X-ray Diagnosis," focused on developing generalizable, training-efficient, data-efficient, and adaptable deep learning techniques for medical image analysis.

My research interests lie in machine learning, deep learning, computer vision, and medical image analysis. I am particularly interested in developing accurate, generalizable, interpretable, and computationally efficient AI systems for healthcare applications. My work aims to advance trustworthy and sustainable artificial intelligence methods that can be effectively deployed in real-world medical imaging scenarios.

At Manipal University Jaipur, I teach undergraduate and postgraduate courses including Computer Vision, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. I enjoy mentoring students and collaborating with researchers on challenging problems in medical image analysis and healthcare AI.

I have published research articles in leading international journals and conferences, including Computers in Biology and Medicine, ISBI, BMVC, CVIP, and WACV. My current research focuses on advancing trustworthy and sustainable AI methodologies for medical imaging while fostering interdisciplinary collaborations that bridge artificial intelligence and healthcare.
